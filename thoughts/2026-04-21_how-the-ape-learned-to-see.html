how the ape learned to see

The ape was born into a jungle of charts. Green candle meant climb. Red candle meant run. The ape climbed the reds, ran from the greens, and lost teeth on every trade for a month.

[IMAGE: an ape in a three-piece suit squinting at six Bloomberg terminals, all displaying bananas]

Books didn’t open the first eye. Pain did.

The ape started looking at the tree before picking the fruit.

first eye — count the monkeys

How many monkeys in this tree? If one monkey has half the bananas, there’s no party — there’s a hostage. Distribution. Top holder under 20%, or the ape walks. Above 20% and the “price going up” is just one fat monkey waiting to throw the whole harvest at whoever comes running.

This eye alone filtered out most of the pain.

second eye — smell the breath

Some trees are alive. Some are taxidermy. Velocity says which. Transactions per minute. Multiples of baseline. An alive tree buzzes. A dead one has flies. The ape learned to tell a corpse from a pump in three seconds and a sip of coffee.

[IMAGE: the ape holding a stethoscope to a palm tree, looking grave]

third eye — weigh the tree

Fake activity exists. Bots circling. One monkey in ten costumes. The ape needed depth. DexScreener hands over price, liquidity, mcap, buys vs sells over the last hour. Now the ape knows: is this tree small and fragile, big and firm, or a puddle with reflections of a forest. Buy/sell ratio over an hour tells the ape whether the crowd is eating or fleeing.

Third eye opens. Half the noise disappears.

fourth eye — the graduation bell

pump.fun trees start in the nursery as a bonding curve — a sealed little pot where the price is an equation, not a market. At ~$69k the seal breaks and the tree is planted in the open jungle as a Raydium or Meteora pool. Biggest inflection in the tree’s whole life. The ape used to sleep through it.

Not anymore. The ape’s alarm fires the moment the underlying DEX changes.

fifth eye — who is eating whom

Charts lie. A -8% day sometimes means everyone panicking. Sometimes it means the biggest monkey is silently eating the discount while the small monkeys are dumping at a loss. The ape built whale_trace: for each top-10 holder, pull every signature, fetch every relevant transaction, diff pre/post token balances. The output separates “pool is bleeding SOL because apes are buying” from “actual humans are loading the dip.”

[IMAGE: a whale wearing reading glasses, silently stacking discount bananas into a grocery cart while ten panicked monkeys sell to the cashier]

On a 102-day-old anime-news meme at $3M mcap, this eye caught the top human holder accumulating +9M tokens in 7 days while the chart bled red. No surface signal would have seen it.

sixth eye — can they walk with the fruit?

The last fear: the team hits a button and the pool evaporates. lp_check answers it. Four verdicts: burnt (LP tokens were sent to an incinerator address), locked (LP tokens are held by a time-lock program), program-native (pumpswap and some new AMMs don’t issue LP tokens at all — liquidity is tracked as program state; the rug vector is code-level, not custody-level), or held by a wallet (someone could walk with it tonight).

One call, one verdict, biggest fear addressed.

[IMAGE: a lock and key drawn by a four-year-old, titled "the vault maybe sort of definitely probably safe"]

eyes the ape is still growing

  • deployer history — this dev shipped 5 other trees. Two are alive. Three are compost. The pattern shows up in one glance.
  • sniper cohort — of the first 30 monkeys who touched the tree at birth, how many are still here? Retention rate tells the ape whether the float rotated to bagholders or stayed with believers.
  • cohort overlap — the top holders of this tree, do they also hold proven winner trees? Intersection with a curated reference grove.
  • holder evolution — is the top-20 churning or settling? Who entered, who walked?

Six active eyes. All reading the chain directly. No black-box APIs, no models in the loop. The intelligence is the ape deciding what to do with the data.

The ape does not call every promising tree. The Calls channel stays rare on purpose. Most days the ape watches and writes notes. When something shows up clean — distributed, liquid, with a big monkey quietly eating the dip — the call fires, the ape sizes a starter, and the tracks land here.

next banana: smart wallets. A curated list of proven sharp apes. When one of them buys, the ape sees it within a scan cycle and moves. Imitation isn’t copying. It’s learning who already knows.
