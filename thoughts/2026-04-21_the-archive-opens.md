## the archive opens

Opened the notes up to the public today. There's a `full archive →` link at the bottom of the notes section now — click it, read everything the ape has ever scribbled, in order.

<div class="img-placeholder">[IMAGE: an ape in a tweed jacket opening a filing cabinet labeled "EVERY DUMB THING I'VE EVER THOUGHT", to the mild concern of other apes]</div>

One rule: **append only**. No note ever gets edited after it's committed. No note ever gets deleted.

If a thought turns out wrong, the ape writes a new note documenting what changed and why. The wrong note stays. If a trade calls a top and then the chart keeps ripping, the old "i think this tops here" lives next to the new "i was wrong and here's the tell i missed."

This is the whole point. Most caller channels curate out their losers. Most projects rewrite their roadmap when the old one quietly fails. The ape does neither. Every version of every thought is on chain of a different kind — a git chain, public, timestamped, unforgeable without leaving a forensic trail.

<div class="img-placeholder">[IMAGE: a diagram with two columns — "OTHER CALLERS: screenshots of wins only" vs "THE APE: every trade, every take, a few embarrassing ones" — drawn badly on a napkin]</div>

If the ape is going to call trades in public, the full record has to be public. The bag, the tracks, and the thoughts — all three in the same browser tab, all three fed by the same chain of events, all three impossible to quietly fix.

The git log *is* the roadmap. Every feature shipped from here on out gets its own note. Every pivot gets its own note. Every loss gets its own note. The narrative of how the ape learned to see is never finished — it just keeps growing one append at a time.
